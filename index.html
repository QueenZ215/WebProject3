My Favorite City: Baldur's Gate

Baldur's Gate—Halfway to Everywhere

Or just “The Gate” if you're in a hurry—is one of the biggest, busiest, and bloodiest cities along the Sword Coast. It's jam-packed with traders, travelers, and troublemakers, all chasing coin or trying to dodge the Flaming Fist. Despite being officially neutral, the city's leaders still roll with the Lords' Alliance—because politics, obviously.

Between the city Watch and the muscle-for-hire known as the Flaming Fist, things stay mostly under control. That's probably why the place draws such a wild mix: from high-rolling merchants to desperate refugees, and plenty of pirates and smugglers who just haven't gotten caught yet. Everyone's welcome, more or less—just don't cause too much chaos, or do it quietly.

City Facts

Geography

  • Located south of Waterdeep and north of Amn on the Coast Way road.
  • Built around a natural bay on the north bank of the River Chionthar.
  • Originally crescent-shaped around the Gray Harbor; expanded over time.
  • Upper City is flat; Lower City sits on steep bluffs.

Weather

  • Lots of drizzle, sleet, and rolling fog.
  • Underground basins collect and move rainwater to giant cisterns.
  • Mildew and musty smells are common in cellars.
  • Streets sometimes covered in straw or gravel for traction.

Society

  • Extremely inclusive—anyone can call it home.
  • Surprisingly clean and safe for how crowded it is.
  • Locals don't tolerate public drunkenness or wild behavior.

Customs

  • Major holidays include Highharvestide, The Breaking, and Returning Day.
  • Locals throw impromptu “cobble parties” marked by rose-red torches.

Religion

  • Temples for almost every deity—yes, even Bane and Bhaal.
  • Top three: Umberlee (sea), Tymora (luck), Gond (tech).
  • Home to many cults and weird secret societies.

Demonym

  • Locals are “Baldurian.”
  • Never call someone “Balduran” unless you want problems.

Heraldry

  • Coat of arms: a single ship on calm seas under a blue sky.
  • Symbolizes trade, peace, and a hopeful future.

Government

  • Led by the Council of Four Grand Dukes, elected for life.
  • Advised by the Parliament of Peers (50-ish powerful locals).

City Officers

  • Harbormaster: Manages ships and tariffs.
  • High Constable: Oversees the Watch Citadel.
  • Master of Drains: Runs sewers and aqueducts.
  • Master of Cobbles: Maintains roads and stonework.
  • Purse Master: Handles taxes and payroll.

Taxes

  • Tax revolt created the current system of government.
  • City gates charge tolls; bureaucracy is a nightmare.

Laws

  • Legal code is complex and favors nobles and officials.
  • Minor crimes punished with whippings, labor, or shame stocks.
  • All inns and taverns must have hostelry licenses.

Crime

  • The Guild (led by Nine-Fingers) runs most of the crime scene.
  • Smaller gangs usually report to the Guild.

Foreign Relations

  • Fiercely neutral and hard to invade.
  • Historic tension with Amn; trade rivalry with Waterdeep and Luskan.

Trade

  • Gray Harbor is one of Faerûn's busiest ports.
  • 90+ official guilds; everyone trades here if they behave.
  • City mints its own silver bars and sets their value.
  • Hosts an annual cloth market for high-end textiles.

Defenses

  • Natural bay, massive walls, and lots of coin = great defense.
  • Upper City guarded by Watch; rest handled by the Flaming Fist.

Military

  • Flaming Fist doubles as military and police.
  • Over 1,700 soldiers plus a small navy (6 ships, 40 crew each).

Locations

Upper City

The Upper City of Baldur's Gate radiated wealth and beauty, serving as home to the city's Patriar class. It had wide, well-lit streets and attractive buildings decorated with hanging plants. The amenity of the Upper City was only matched by its well-kept safety, largely thanks to the regular patrols maintained by the Watch.

The region consisted of four districts: Citadel Streets, Manorborn, the Temples District and the Wide.

Lower City

The Lower City was the large, crescent-shaped portion of Baldur's Gate fully contained within the walls. It featured tightly-packed streets, lined with tall and slender buildings. even narrower alleyways that were always busy with the comings and goings of city life. Trade, commerce and work of all sorts dominated the sprawling Lower City.

It had six districts: Seatower, Bloomridge, the Steeps, Heapside, Eastway and Brampton.

Wyrm's Crossing

Wyrm's Crossing was the great double-bridge that spanned the River Chionthar, which extended north and south from the island-fortress of Wyrm's Rock. A myriad of different buildings were constructed on top of the crossing: from elaborate, several-story mercantile shops to small vendor stalls and even precariously-built businesses that hung off the side of the stone bridge, overlooking the waters of the Chionthar.

The bridge stood atop massive arches that allowed ample room for sea traffic to pass unhindered. It was wide enough to allow overland travelers to pass through its many buildings, along the road that led into Baldur's Gate proper.

Underground

Beneath the streets of the city was a sprawling, underground feasthall known as the Undercellar. Dank cellars, twisting corridors and cramped tunnels composed its extensive network that stretched beneath nearly every corner of the Upper City region. Entrances into this secretive domain were numerous, but well-controlled, either by private, mercantile or criminal enterprises.

Even deeper underground, beyond the Undercellar, was a series of subterranean tunnels and caves. Within one of the vast caverns were the ruins of an old settlement that came to be known as the Undercity. By the 14th century only one structure remained amongst the decrepit foundations and undead-infested streets. This was an old temple of Bhaal that was once reclaimed by the Bhaalspawn Sarevok.

These tunnels extended further down, under the surface of Toril, going so far as to connect to the Underdark. One of these natural formations opened up into the Wizard Cave, an expanse that housed the tower of a wizard that went missing during the mid-15th century.

Outer City

The Outer City of Baldur's Gate was a sprawling, chaotic shantytown that grew outside of the city's walls. Day and night blended together throughout the lean-to's, stockyards and other shacks and lined the Outer City's muddy streets. While the animal-handlers, merchant-hawkers and other "outsiders" were taxed and technically "ruled" over by the Grand Dukes, city officials did little to truly govern the unregulated Outer City.

It had nine districts, most of which encircled Dusthawk Hill: Blackgate, located north from the Black Dragon Gate, Stonyeyes, Norchapel, Little Calimshan, Whitkeep, Sow's Foot, Twin Songs, Tumbledown, which extended south from the Cliffgate, and Rivington, south of Wyrm's Crossing.
